# PRESENTATION_NOTES

## 1. 30-second project summary
This project is called **Museum of Cold War Propaganda**. It is a digital museum website built with Next.js, TypeScript, and Tailwind CSS. The main idea is to present Cold War propaganda not just as historical artifacts, but as a system of persuasion. Instead of making the site feel like a generic app or blog, I designed it as a curated exhibition with a strong visual identity, a clear interpretive voice, and a documented AI-assisted workflow.

## 2. Museum concept explanation
The concept behind the project is that propaganda is more useful to study when it is treated as a set of techniques rather than as isolated objects. So the site compares how the United States and the Soviet Union used posters, radio, and broadcast media to shape belief, authority, fear, and public behavior. The goal is to help visitors see the mechanics behind the media, not just the historical surface.

## 3. Why I chose the design style
I chose a Swiss design style because it fits the project academically and visually. Swiss design is structured, restrained, and typographically clear. That helped me make the site feel serious and exhibition-like instead of decorative. It also supports the museum idea because it lets the content carry the weight rather than relying on effects or visual noise.

## 4. How I used the persuasion principle
The Cialdini principle I used was **Authority**. I applied that by making the site feel credible and research-driven. The writing uses a museum-style voice, the pages emphasize dates and institutional context, and the process page makes the method visible. I wanted the visitor to feel that the site was making a careful argument, not just presenting opinions.

## 5. How the brand archetype shaped the site
The brand archetype is **Sage**. That shaped the tone of the project more than anything else. I wrote the site in a calm, analytical, interpretive voice. I avoided sensational language, and I tried to make each page feel educational and reflective. The Sage archetype helped keep the project focused on explanation instead of dramatization.

## 6. How I used AI in my workflow
I used AI as a structured design and writing assistant, not as a substitute for decision-making. It helped me scaffold the site, refine page structure, improve writing, and document my process. But the important part is that the AI work was constrained by specs, reviews, and sprint documentation, so it stayed aligned to the project instead of wandering.

## 7. My orchestration process
The workflow I followed was:

**Spec → QA → Sprint → QA → Implement → QA**

I started by defining the site foundation, including scope, tone, design framework, and acceptance criteria. Then I reviewed that spec for ambiguity and scope drift. After that, I created a Sprint 1 document to define what a realistic midterm version should include. Then I used QA again to evaluate the first-review build, revised the site, and documented what still needs improvement. That process helped me show not just what I built, but how I built it.

## 8. How the site was reviewed as an exhibition
I did not review the project only as a website. I also reviewed it as a museum exhibition. That means I looked at narrative flow, exhibit organization, artifact presentation, educational value, visual coherence, and visitor reading rhythm. In other words, I asked whether the pages felt like connected gallery rooms instead of unrelated screens.

## 9. What is strong already
I think the strongest parts right now are the conceptual clarity, the visual consistency, and the process documentation. The homepage, posters page, broadcast page, and process page now feel like parts of the same exhibition. The project also has a clear design framework and a documented workflow, which makes it stronger as a class project because the reasoning is visible.

## 10. What I plan to improve before final review
Before the final review, I want to tighten some of the copy so the reading rhythm is even better, improve accessibility and mobile readability, and make artifact-level source signaling more explicit where needed. I also want to keep refining the exhibit pages so they feel even more polished as museum spaces, while still staying restrained.

## 11. A short closing statement
Overall, this midterm version is meant to show that I have a strong concept, a coherent system, and a serious process. It is not the final polished version yet, but it demonstrates the direction of the project and gives me a clear foundation for the final review.
